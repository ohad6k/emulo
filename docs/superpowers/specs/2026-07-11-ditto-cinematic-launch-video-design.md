# Ditto Cinematic Launch Video Design

## Objective

Create a 20-second, 16:9 launch film for X that makes Ditto feel like a premium cinematic artifact rather than a software explainer. The film must preserve Ditto's Roman engraving identity while telling a clear story about an AI agent becoming a faithful mirror of the person using it.

The reference bar is the supplied 9.5-second montage: dense shot changes, macro-to-wide camera jumps, shape-based match cuts, minimal text, tactile texture, and edits controlled by music and sound design. The reference's live-action footage will not be copied or reused.

## Locked Creative Direction

**Living Engraving Prophecy.** A Roman-era human and a mechanical mirror twin inhabit one continuous illustrated world. The visual language is an animated historical engraving shot like a modern trailer, not a set of illustrated panels.

This direction is preferable to simulated live action because it allows exact control over character continuity, camera choreography, lighting, material texture, and the human/agent mirror metaphor. It also grows directly from Ditto's existing engraving instead of introducing a second, less trustworthy brand language.

## Non-Negotiable Quality Rules

- No slideshow structure, title-card sequence, floating UI cards, diagrams, browser chrome, or feature-list layouts.
- Every moment is a full-frame shot inside one continuous world.
- The Roman human and mechanical twin remain recognizable throughout the film.
- Each edit is motivated by a camera move, object movement, lighting change, impact, or shape-based match cut.
- No generic neon AI imagery, random particles, sci-fi holograms, glossy SaaS gradients, or synthetic stock-photo look.
- Text is limited to three short beats and is integrated into the environment as ink, inscription, or final lockup.
- The film remains understandable with audio muted, but music and sound design provide the primary pacing.
- No voiceover.

## Format and Delivery

- Duration: 20.0 seconds, with a tolerance of 0.1 seconds.
- Master: 1920×1080, 60 fps, H.264 video, AAC stereo audio.
- Platform: X feed, optimized for immediate interruption, replayability, and legibility on mobile.
- Visual safe area: all load-bearing text stays within the center 80 percent of frame width and 82 percent of frame height.
- Audio target: approximately -14 LUFS integrated, with true peak at or below -1 dBTP.

## Story

A Roman man confronts a mechanical twin in a dark mirror. The twin initially copies only his shape. Their contact releases the man's buried working history as manuscript fragments. Noise is stripped away, repeated traits are inscribed into a single `you.md`, and the artifact enters the mechanical twin. The twin then mirrors the human with perfect timing. Ditto has turned resemblance into understanding.

The film uses 15 shots. Average shot length is roughly 1.3 seconds, with faster cuts during the archive transformation and longer holds for the opening hook and final brand lockup.

## Shot Architecture

### Movement I: The Empty Copy, 0.0–4.0s

1. **0.0–0.8s:** Black. A metallic eye aperture snaps open on the first impact. Extreme macro, shallow engraved depth, red seal reflection.
2. **0.8–1.7s:** Shape match to a Roman human eye cut into parchment and marble texture. A torch reflection moves across the pupil.
3. **1.7–2.8s:** Fast pullback reveals the Roman man facing a polished obsidian mirror inside a vast, shadowed archive chamber.
4. **2.8–4.0s:** The reflection is a bronze mechanical twin. It moves one beat late. Environmental inscription appears: **YOUR AI FORGETS YOU.**

### Movement II: The Buried History, 4.0–9.0s

5. **4.0–5.0s:** The human raises his hand. The camera orbits toward the mirror while the twin follows imperfectly.
6. **5.0–5.8s:** Fingers touch across the black surface. A bass impact fractures the mirror into concentric engraved rings.
7. **5.8–7.0s:** The camera dives through the fracture into a storm of session manuscripts, command traces, file dumps, and user-written lines.
8. **7.0–8.0s:** Tool noise burns to pale ash while the user's words remain as dense black ink ribbons.
9. **8.0–9.0s:** The ribbons whip past camera and become orbiting inscriptions around the mechanical twin's head. Text beat: **YOUR HISTORY DOESN'T.**

### Movement III: The Mirror Learns, 9.0–15.8s

10. **9.0–10.4s:** Repeating traits strike into bronze like minting dies: proof, taste, scope, done. One-off fragments fall away.
11. **10.4–11.8s:** The camera follows an ink line as it folds into a single illuminated manuscript sheet.
12. **11.8–13.0s:** The filename `you.md` is inscribed into the sheet. The document compresses into a narrow brass-and-ink artifact.
13. **13.0–14.3s:** The artifact enters the twin's chest. Engraved pathways ignite from chest to eye, restrained to brass, black, parchment, and dark red.
14. **14.3–15.8s:** Hard match cut back to the mirror chamber. Human and twin turn their heads in perfect synchronization for the first time.

### Movement IV: Ditto, 15.8–20.0s

The final movement is one continuous hero shot rather than another explanatory scene. The camera pulls back to reveal both profiles aligned as the existing Ditto engraving. The chamber, mirror, and manuscript fragments collapse into the brand plate. The music reaches its final hit at 17.2 seconds. The lockup resolves in two stages:

- **17.2–18.2s:** `Ditto`
- **18.2–20.0s:** **STOP STARTING FROM ZERO** and `github.com/ohad6k/ditto`

The last frame holds long enough to read and should loop cleanly back toward the opening black frame.

## Visual System

### Characters

- **Human:** a single Roman-era male profile rendered as a living copperplate engraving, dignified rather than heroic or militaristic. No costume spectacle, laurel clichés, eagles, or generic columns.
- **Agent:** a mirrored bronze automaton derived from the machine half of the existing Ditto engraving. It uses circular mechanical anatomy and engraved brass, not modern robot armor.
- **Continuity:** character proportions, face, hair, profile angle, and core mechanical geometry remain stable. New scene art must be derived from a single approved master reference rather than generated independently.

### Materials and Light

- Parchment, black ink, aged bronze, marble dust, obsidian mirror, and a restrained dark-red seal accent.
- Directional torchlight and hard shafts of light create photographic contrast inside the illustrated world.
- Grain, ink bleed, plate scratches, and paper fibers remain visible at macro scale.
- Depth comes from layered engraving planes, controlled defocus, volumetric light, and parallax, not drop shadows or floating cards.

### Camera Language

- Extreme macro details alternate with architectural wides.
- Fast optical pulls, orbit moves, push-throughs, and object-following camera moves create trailer energy.
- At least one meaningful camera or subject event occurs every 1.5 seconds.
- Match cuts reuse circles, eyes, rings, hands, profiles, and manuscript lines as transition geometry.
- Motion blur, depth of field, and lens distortion are restrained and deterministic.

### Typography

- Display text uses the existing editorial serif system in sentence-independent, monumental compositions.
- `you.md` and the repository URL use a quiet monospace face.
- Text never arrives inside a box. It is stamped, engraved, revealed by light, or resolved as part of the final plate.

## Music and Sound Design

The score is an original or safely licensed hybrid of dark orchestral percussion and industrial rhythm, approximately 145–155 BPM. It has no vocals and no melodic sentimentality.

- **0.0–4.0s:** low sub pulse, metal tension, sparse heartbeat-like strikes.
- **4.0–9.0s:** percussion accelerates as the archive fractures open.
- **9.0–15.8s:** clockwork rhythm and low strings build while traits are minted and `you.md` forms.
- **15.8–20.0s:** rhythm drops briefly, then returns with one final orchestral-industrial hit and a short tail.

Sound effects include eye aperture, torch movement, cloth or paper movement, mirror contact, fracture, ink whip, ash fall, minting strikes, manuscript fold, brass insertion, synchronization click, and final seal impact. SFX must feel embedded in the score rather than layered as novelty sounds.

## Production Approach

1. Preserve the existing Ditto engraving as the identity anchor and final brand plate.
2. Create a single master character/reference plate for the Roman human and mechanical twin.
3. Derive only the minimum additional scene plates needed for the mirror chamber, hand contact, archive storm, and twin activation.
4. Separate plates into foreground, subject, atmosphere, and background layers for 2.5D animation.
5. Build one root HyperFrames composition with continuous timing and shot-level sub-compositions only where isolation improves control.
6. Use seek-safe deterministic animation for all camera, mask, ink, light, and depth effects.
7. Resolve or create music and SFX as frozen local assets and record them in the media ledger.
8. Mix and loudness-normalize audio before the final render.

## Failure Handling

- If generated scene art changes the character identity, reject it and reuse or transform the master plate instead of accepting inconsistency.
- If a transition reads as a slide change, replace it with a match cut or continuous camera move.
- If a frame requires more than one sentence to understand, simplify the visual event rather than adding labels.
- If the music does not provide a clean 20-second structure, edit it to the film's four movements before animating to it.
- If 60 fps rendering is unsupported or unstable, render lossless image frames at 60 fps and assemble them with FFmpeg rather than lowering the delivery target without review.

## Verification and Acceptance

The video is complete only when all of the following evidence exists:

- HyperFrames lint, validate, and inspect complete without errors.
- Final MP4 probes as 1920×1080, 60 fps, H.264 with AAC stereo, and 19.9–20.1 seconds.
- Audio loudness analysis is near -14 LUFS with true peak no higher than -1 dBTP.
- A contact sheet covers the entire 20-second timeline and shows a genuine sequence of shots rather than repeated layouts.
- Opening hook, mirror reveal, archive transformation, `you.md` formation, synchronized twin, and final CTA are all visibly present.
- No load-bearing text is clipped at 16:9 or illegible in a mobile-scale preview.
- A full playback review confirms there are no frozen frames, missing assets, timeline jumps, unmotivated transitions, or visual continuity breaks.
- The final frame holds the brand and URL for at least 1.5 seconds.
- The film can be understood muted and gains impact when played with sound.
