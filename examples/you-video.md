# Emulo video profile

> This is an example seed rendered in the exact shape `emulo` emits for the `video`
> domain. The live copy at `~/.emulo/profiles/default/versions/<ver>/you-video.md`
> is regenerated by mining your own sessions and only activates once there is enough
> video evidence. The rules below are distilled from a real BiosRios build session.

- Accent is set by the reference clip, not by exaggeration alone; a cloned voice drifts into a wrong accent when the reference is over filtered and guidance is low.
  - Action: Clone from a clean, minimally processed reference (loudnorm only, 24k mono, no bandpass or EQ); keep cfg 0.65 to 0.7 and temperature 0.45, and carry energy with exaggeration 0.42 to 0.6 per line.
- Accumulating on-screen lines overlap and throw render errors when placed as multiple clips on one track.
  - Action: Reveal each accumulating line as a plain div driven by the timeline, never as its own clip on a shared track.
- Build every video as a single paused GSAP timeline in HyperFrames, root carrying the composition id and data-start, data-duration, data-width, data-height, with every animated element marked as a clip.
  - Action: Author one seek-safe paused timeline; gate every reveal to its beat so a static seek renders correctly.
- A GSAP fromTo in a timeline applies its from state at build time, so any from state that is visible paints over the whole video from frame 0.
  - Action: Set gsap.defaults immediateRender false in every composition; a white flash tween or a hide-the-previous-pose tween otherwise washes or clutters every frame.
- A logo-head agent figure disappears when the suit silhouette is near-black on a near-black canvas, leaving a floating head over a collar.
  - Action: Draw the suit light enough to read on the background with a faint outline, crop the artwork to the torso so the neck sits at its top edge, and overlap the head onto the neck.
- Captions carry one idea per scene and match the voiceover word for word.
  - Action: Write uppercase display captions of 2 to 4 words that repeat the spoken words exactly, popped in with a back.out ease.
- Copy for a video is Ohad's voice: normal capitalization, no em or en dashes, no trust-badge filler, real numbers when Emulo is the subject.
  - Action: Use 1,656 sessions, 9 months, 3M tokens where Emulo is the subject; end on the felt thing, never a guessed claim.
- Each voiceover line has its own energy, not one global setting; calm explanatory lines slur less, punchy lines carry the hook.
  - Action: Tag calm lines exaggeration 0.42 to 0.5 cfg 0.68 to 0.7, and hook, reveal, and CTA lines exaggeration 0.6 cfg 0.65.
- Emoji reactions read cheap.
  - Action: Replace them with full-screen character-face cut-ins: a brief white flash, a slam-in expression, a spinning burst, and one outlined caption line.
- Every voiceover line must be understandable; high exaggeration slurs words a transcriber then mishears.
  - Action: Run a clarity gate per line: generate up to 3 takes, transcribe each with whisper, keep the best match that fits its slot, and regenerate anything the transcriber mishears.
- Mixing a from() entrance and a later to() y tween on the same element teleports it under seek-render.
  - Action: Nest wrappers so each owns one transform: outer for opacity and position, a react layer for y, a bob layer for bob and tilt, then the image.
- Never speed audio past about 1.15x or it chipmunks, and moving line offsets forces a re-render.
  - Action: Pad each line with silence back to its fixed timeline slot so offsets never move; rebuild the final mix and remux instead of re-rendering.
- One plugin or item card is on screen at a time, with its number inside the card and no floating badges.
  - Action: Center a single card, put the number inside it, and dim the background so the card reads.
- Reaction cut-ins and everything on screen must keep continuous looped motion for their whole window; a static hold reads as a render bug.
  - Action: Loop bob, wobble, breathing, and burst spin for the full duration with repeats sized to the window; never animate-in then hold.
- The PowerPoint feel is measurable, not a vibe: a four-minute cut that "felt static" had 76 freezedetect runs totaling 103 of 247 seconds, and its motion file held 28 tweens with only 2 repeats.
  - Action: Diagnose staticness as frozen-seconds over duration before touching design; fix it with one keepAlive layer that gives every scene a slow full-window camera push plus sized loops on caption, character, and props, rather than restyling anything.
- Continuous motion alone does not kill the slide feel if the video still cuts every 1 to 3 seconds; the reference grammar cuts fast only in the hook and then holds one visual world 5 to 15 seconds per beat, and ffmpeg scene detection makes the difference visible (the reference's cuts cluster in the first 10s with 6 to 15s gaps after, the slide-feel cut had 40 evenly spaced full-frame changes in 120s at 1.67s average shot length).
  - Action: Compare cut cadence with scdet before styling; a per-phrase shot list is the disease, so merge each narrative beat into one persistent scene world around its most media-rich shot.
- Inside a held world the phrases accumulate instead of cutting: captions slam-swap in place, a rapid list becomes one note card gaining lines, the host changes pose in place, and a kinetic line is a brief full-frame cut-away that returns to the same world.
  - Action: Render later phrases as phase layers on the world (caption swap, note lines, pose crossfade, opaque slam cut-away) and never rebuild the frame within a beat; a source like a tweet enters once per beat and evolves, never re-enters.
- Two tweens animating the same transform on one element read as a bug, not motion; a cutaway with a cut-settle plus a keepAlive push plus its own camera push looked "buggy, not clean" on every jump.
  - Action: Give every element exactly ONE owner per transform — a cutaway's camera is its own tween (a punch-in decaying into a drift), with the generic settle and keepAlive push skipped for it; cut into photo material on a one-frame flash, which is what makes a hard cut read as edited rather than broken.
- The open is the whole video's audition and cannot be a normal scene; a static host shot for the first seconds loses the click.
  - Action: Flag the first ~13s as hook shots and give them their own treatment: sweeping colour bars, a host punch-in, the hardest camera push in the video, and the first literal cutaway inside the first 4 seconds.
- A camera step on every text change reads as an annoying jump zoom, not energy; he flagged it within one viewing.
  - Action: Give each world ONE continuous slow push (ease none, full window) and let text land with its own entrance animation only; never tie a zoom to a caption change.
- Boxed caption cards (cream fill, orange border, drop shadow) are the single strongest vibe-coded-PowerPoint tell, and an invented mock screen ("agent session") that viewers do not recognize is the second.
  - Action: Set text directly on the canvas: white words with a soft shadow over dark worlds, ink words over light ones, and full-screen white-on-black centered lines as transition beats; when the concept is prompting, show the real Claude app window (cream, coral ball, typed prompt, streaming reply), never an invented terminal board.
- A repeated identical wrapper is THE vibe-coded fingerprint: the same dark frame box, the same mono metadata tag top-right, and the same caption band on every screen read as a template even when each screen's content differs; one tag even leaked an internal line id onto the screen.
  - Action: Show screens as raw pinned artifacts (white mat, shadow, slight tilt, straight on the textured canvas); attribution is a handwritten chalk credit and exists only for real sources; internal ids, "reaction cam" labels, and per-screen metadata chrome never render.
- The channel's subject is teaching people AI (plugins, workflows, new models, skills, how to create things), never coding for its own sake; the traffic lives in learn-AI content, in two registers: the funny build and the straight teach.
  - Action: Pitch every video as what a non-coder can DO afterwards; keep the roast energy but aim it at AI workflows, and let code appear only as the vehicle.
- Real product surfaces are captured, never drawn: hyperframes capture pulls a live site's real fonts, colors, assets and design tokens to disk for free in about 30 seconds (claude.ai yields Anthropic Sans, Serif, Mono and its #121212 ink), and the creator never records anything because the agent drives the capture.
  - Action: Capture every product the video discusses and build its on-screen surface from the captured tokens; treat a hand-drawn approximation as the thing viewers read as vibe-coded, and re-capture per video rather than cloning one old demo.
- One product is one surface: Claude's web chat and Claude Code's terminal are different products, and putting a CLI moment (plan mode) inside the cream web window produced a hybrid that matched neither and made every screen feel like the same template.
  - Action: Give each product its own faithful surface (cream window for the web app, dark box-drawn terminal for the CLI) and pick the surface by which product actually hosts that moment.
- Subtitles must never depend on what is behind them; chalk script vanished entirely over the cream Claude window.
  - Action: Set subtitles in plain heavy white with a thick dark stroke and shadow so they read on any background, and reserve decorative faces for transition cards only; specificity beats !important, which the font resolver cannot parse.
- A product mock earns realism by re-enacting recognizable product MOMENTS, not one generic interaction; the plan-mode approval prompt ("Would you like to proceed? 1. Yes, and auto-accept edits") is one of many.
  - Action: For each narration beat that touches a product, pick the actual UX moment its users see daily (plan approval, tool cards with diff counts, streaming answers, permission prompts) and rotate them; never put explanatory text on a literal cutaway, never letterbox real media in bars, and never run an SFX longer than ~0.8s under speech.
- A drawn card floating on an empty dark background IS the vibe-coded look, even styled cream; the reference (Coding Sloth) never floats a card — the real product screen FILLS the frame edge to edge, and big outlined text plus meme cutouts sit on top of it.
  - Action: Make every explainer surface full-bleed and real (a leaderboard as a windowed app screenshot with ranked rows and real logos, an X feed as a real light-mode timeline), never a small panel on a dark field; layer punchy Anton text and photo-meme cutaways over the real screen.
- A visual element hidden by an unanimated parent renders blank even when its own tweens are correct; the waveform bars had the right scaleY but their #wave container sat at CSS opacity 0 with nothing turning it on, so the whole chapter looked empty.
  - Action: When a beat renders empty, check the container opacity first, not the child animation; every element that starts at opacity 0 in CSS needs an explicit fade-in tween.
- Real footage is a timed top-level media element, never nested and never relying on the loop attribute for length: a 3s clip stretched to 23s captures ~13 percent of frames and renders blank past a coverage gate.
  - Action: Give each footage clip its own id and data-start/duration as a top-level element (scrim as a separate sibling div), and physically loop-concatenate the source to exceed its on-screen window before rendering.
- Compare the exact competing MODELS, never a brand: the flagship is Claude Fable 5, and pairing "Sol vs Claude" with Opus 4.8's number (78.9) instead of Fable 5's (84.3 on Terminal-Bench 2.1) is a factual error a viewer catches.
  - Action: Name the specific model on screen and in the voice (Fable 5, not "Claude"), verify each score against two sources, and show the full ranked board so the comparison is honest.
- A short built fast reads as empty: text-on-black with the character absent for the open and long stretches, plus a real product's wordmark where viewers expect its icon (the OpenAI blossom, not "OpenAI" text), and z-layers that let a cutaway, a lesson card, and a caption all paint at once.
  - Action: Keep the host on screen from frame 1 to the kicker with beat-matched poses; fill the open with real stacked artifacts (overlapping tweet cards for "everyone posted the same screenshot") instead of empty black; use each product's recognizable ICON; and give cutaways the top z-index with an explicit hide of any lesson or panel underneath so only one big element shows at a time.
- A full-screen stock photo every time is its own template; the reference composites instead — a widget lands ON the scene, and the joke is built from parts rather than found in a photo.
  - Action: Give cutaways presentation modes and vary them: full-bleed only for the big beats, otherwise a card that punches onto the scene (tilted, white border, shadow, over a blurred backdrop or the live world). Composite the joke with real UI elements (a login card and a map pin dropped onto a Lisbon street beats a clean tram photo); a generated photo is a backdrop for the gag, not the gag.
- The layer that separates an edited video from a themed slideshow is LITERAL association: when the voice says crime scene the screen shows police tape, Portugal shows a Lisbon tram, cheating shows a CHEATER arcade screen, breaking shows a glitched 404; the reference channels do this on nearly every concrete noun.
  - Action: Extract the concrete nouns from the script and give each key one a full-bleed photorealistic cutaway (generated stills read as stock footage) with one big outlined word, shown as a brief takeover at the exact moment the word is spoken; a generated image that fails to save may still exist in the generator's output directory, so read the error before regenerating.
- A UI mock that performs the same interaction every appearance (typing into the input, seven times) reads as a loop, not a product.
  - Action: Give every recurring mock modes and rotate them deterministically: type the prompt, stream a real worded answer, and act with tool cards (Edit diffs, a test run landing green); a mock earns its screen time by DOING something new each visit.
- Frame-watching the reference (via the watch skill) revealed texture rules scene detection cannot see: exactly two text registers (bold serif, sentence case with a period, on a FLAT gray or black card for transitions; white chalk handwriting for in-world labels and hand-boxed tool names), dark worlds carry a cloudy chalkboard texture rather than flat black, collage scenes sit on real photographic backdrops, and most content is real artifacts pinned as cards (real chat answers, tweets, docs pages, diagrams beside hand-drawn sketches).
  - Action: Watch the reference with frame extraction before matching a style, then reproduce its texture layer: serif transition cards on flat dark, chalk labels over chalkboard-textured worlds, real screenshots pinned as cards, and photo backdrops behind collage beats; measured cadence alone misses all of this.
- Caption elements styled edge-to-edge collide as BOXES even when the words are short, which reads as the host overlapping text.
  - Action: Captions hug their text (fit-content, margin-based centering, never translateX under GSAP) and layout audits must measure real element boxes; in screen worlds give the window a side rail so the host and notes have a home that is not on top of the UI.
- A project's quality gates encode the editing grammar, so after a direction change the old gates fail the new grammar and silently push work back toward the rejected look.
  - Action: When the grammar changes, retune every gate to the new spec in the same pass (streak limits, character share, event rate, shot counts), each with a comment naming the grammar it now tests.
- Merging shots into one world puts elements on screen together that never coexisted, so the layout checker starts catching real collisions the per-shot layout hid.
  - Action: After any merge, run the layout check and fix placement structurally: the host shrinks to a corner commentator in screen worlds but keeps his authored side, notes avoid the side the world's own text uses, and anything intentionally covering the frame must actually hide what is under it.
- Regenerating any voice line silently invalidates every timing artifact downstream, and a stale transcript drifts the visuals a full sentence off the audio by mid-video.
  - Action: Treat VO as the head of a chain (narration assembly, line timing, shot data, captions or srt, sfx cues, final mix) and rebuild the whole chain in order after any line changes; also update any hand-carried times, like act boundaries in the root composition.
- Long-format is the priority and it is a teaching video, not a stretched short: one concept per screen, sustained explanation, a real structure of chapters.
  - Action: Author long videos at 1920x1080 around 3 to 4 minutes; open on a cinematic hook, then walk one idea at a time with the character hosting and reacting between concepts.
- The long-format canvas is mostly pure black with big centered ELEGANT SERIF text, one idea per screen, not the Anton bold of the shorts.
  - Action: Put a single serif line (EB Garamond or similar) centered on near-black for each beat; reserve the heavy Anton block captions for the shorts.
- Teaching a concept needs the real thing on screen, not just words: real product screenshots, tool UIs, and diagrams carry the explanation.
  - Action: Show real Claude, Claude Code, and tool UI screenshots plus diagrams (agent workflow node graphs, architecture trees) as each concept lands; never explain a mechanism with text alone.
- AI agents read best as a visual meme: suited men in black with AI-logo heads (a coral Claude sunburst head, others), labeled by role.
  - Action: Represent agents as suited figures with logo heads labeled planner, researcher, builder; use the coral Claude sunburst for the Claude agent.
- Shorts and long videos share the same house identity (character, dark orange palette, cloned voice) but different canvas, pacing, and text weight, so confirm aspect and format per platform.
  - Action: Author shorts at 1080x1920 with punchy Anton captions and long videos at 1920x1080 with serif teaching cards; the same cloned voice and final-mix pipeline serve both. Reference for the long format: TheCodingSloth "Everything You Need To Know About AI Agents".
- The dark house style is near-black with a warm vignette, orange is the whole channel, and coral belongs only inside the Claude UI mock.
  - Action: Use a #0a0a0a background with a radial vignette, accent #ff6b35 everywhere, and #d97757 only inside the cream Claude window.
- The hook is tension in frame 1, never a topic title; on a low-sub channel every frame earns the push on hook and retention alone.
  - Action: Open on a wound, a number, or a mistake in the first frame, not a title card; keep the lead-in under about 0.6s so no dead black opens the retention graph.
- Freezes are provable, not a matter of opinion, and the end card is the easiest one to miss because its motion is authored to a fixed length.
  - Action: Run ffmpeg freezedetect over every finished cut and treat any reported run as a bug; carry the closing motion all the way to the composition total, not a hardcoded few seconds.
- A brand name is slurred by energy and by the words in front of it, not by its spelling; emulo lands cleanly at exaggeration 0.5 in the frame "a tool called emulo" but becomes AMOLO after "why I built" and MLue at exaggeration 0.6.
  - Action: Speak proper nouns at exaggeration 0.42 to 0.5, never hook energy, and put them in a "called X" frame; fix a mangled name by changing the energy and the carrier phrase, never by respelling it phonetically, since every respelling only produces a different wrong word.
- Sentence-initial is the fragile slot for any odd word, not just brand names; a line opening on "Vibe coding" decoded as Live, 5, wipe, and why across takes, and landed only with a carrier word in front.
  - Action: Never open a line with the word that must land; give it a short carrier ("Now, vibe coding...") and gate that token exactly under base.en.
- tiny.en scores a mangled proper noun as clear, so the clarity gate silently passes a wrong brand name.
  - Action: Gate any line containing a product name with whisper base.en and assert the name appears as an exact token in the transcript, rejecting the take outright when it does not.
- The transcriber writes spoken numbers as digits ("Level one" comes back "Level 1"), so a word-token gate rejects perfect takes of any line containing a number.
  - Action: Token asserts must accept digit equivalents (one/1, two/2, three/3); when a batch fails the gate, read the actual transcripts before regenerating, because a false gate failure wastes a full CPU batch.
- The roast-plus-guide video is the LEVELS format: demonstrate the dumb way on a real screen, laugh at its real output, then teach the level above and show its result; the creator roasts his own failure modes, never the audience.
  - Action: Structure mixed-take videos as Level 1 (pure vibes, real broken demo), Level 2 (boundaries, the passing request), Level 3 (receipts); open on personal credibility ("I do this every day and it keeps breaking my apps") and end on when to use each level.
- Act groupings that use fixed scene counts silently drop every scene past the block size when a script grows; 46 seconds of visuals fell out of the acts without any error.
  - Action: Group acts by chapter (the semantic unit), assert the chapter count matches the sub-composition count, and fail loudly on mismatch.
- A line can be clean in the voice track and still be smeared in the final mix, because a short sidechain release lets the music swell back over the last syllable; emulo survived the take but became emulog once the bed was under it.
  - Action: Set the music duck release to about 750ms so it outlasts a word tail, and always re-verify the important line by transcribing it back out of the finished mix, not just the voice track; the mix is audio only, so fixing it costs a remux and never a re-render.
- A word the transcriber keeps mishearing is a real ambiguity for viewers too; tax came back as text in 3 of 4 takes.
  - Action: When a take is clean but a word is still misheard, change the word rather than reroll; cost for tax scored 1.00 immediately.
- A rename can land while a video is in flight, so the product name in a cut goes stale silently.
  - Action: Before every render, re-read the install command and product name from the repo README and manifests rather than from memory or an earlier session.
- The install CTA and handle must be exact; a guessed command on a public video is a failure.
  - Action: Show /plugin install emulo@emulo or npx skills add ohad6k/emulo@emulo, never a pip guess, and end on the @biosrios handle.
- The mascot is the channel identity: a polished manga boy, white spiky hair, white hoodie, reading as solid white on black where the light fills are the character.
  - Action: Use 5 to 6 poses per short matched to beats, placed large and moving from center to the sides; never invert him to hollow outlines.
- The on-screen content is the real Claude app, alive, not a generic IDE.
  - Action: Replicate the cream window, the coral ball, the serif greeting with an italic name, the input card, the pill chips, and the microcopy, and make it type, blink, send, and stream word by word; recolor the Claude logo to coral, never black.
- The quality bar is high-energy creator editing but cleaner, carried by the audio mix.
  - Action: Mix voice, a music bed, and sound-effect hits into one final mix, then remux without re-rendering video; keep the bed low, add frequent transition and impact hits, and loudness-normalize.
- Voice generation must not touch the local machine-learning environment and the watermarker fails to init on some machines.
  - Action: Run the cloned-voice model in an isolated venv and stub the watermarker before importing it.
